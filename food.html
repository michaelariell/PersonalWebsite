Aceh's Foods

1. Mie Aceh

Mi aceh is a noodle spicy Aceh in Indonesia. Thick yellow noodles with sliced ​​beef, mutton or seafood (shrimp and squid) served in a savory and spicy curry-type soup. Mi aceh is usually sprinkled with fried onions and served with emping, chopped shallots, cucumber, and lime.

2. Sie Reuboh

Sie reuboh is one of special culinary specialties, precisely from Aceh Besar. This food has become a mandatory dish for local people when they have a lot of meat supplies. For example, at the time of Eid al-Adha and Meugang. One of the characteristics of this dish is that it uses gapah or fat as a natural ingredient so that the food will last longer. In addition, this recipe also uses a variety of original spices and seasonings from Aceh. The taste of sie reuboh is very delicious, spicy, savory, and also sour. culinary has always been a prima donna for the local people of Aceh and tourists who are visiting.

3. Keumamah

Keumamah is one of the traditional Acehnese made from fish raw materials, namely tuna and skipjack tuna. Keumamah is also known as wooden fish because it is hard like wood. This fish is preserved by several manufacturing processes. Starting from fish cleaning, boiling, drying and storage. Therefore keumamah can be stored for years on condition that it must be kept dry or not damp.

4. Sate Matang

Cooked satay is meat form of satay in the province Aceh. This satay is called mature satay because it was originally introduced by the seller in the town of Matang Geuleumpang Dua, a sub-district town in Bireuen district. In the 1990s this satay was popular in several big cities in Aceh, now there are a lot of satay sellers in Aceh and Medan North Sumatra that sells satay with the label "cooked satay". The main ingredient in cooked satay is mutton, due to the higher price of mutton, often cooked satay is made using beef. The process of making and cooking cooked satay is not much different from satay from other regions in Indonesia, the pieces of meat that have been cleaned and cut into small pieces. After being pinned on a skewer, then soaked in a mixture of spices in the form of spices for a long time. Next, the satay is ready to be grilled.
