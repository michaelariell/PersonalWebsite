Banda Aceh

The city of Banda Aceh is the capital of NAD Province. Previously this city was named Kutaraja, then since December 28, 1962 its name was changed to Banda Aceh. As the center of government, the 803-year-old City of Banda Aceh (based on Aceh Regional Regulation No.5/1988, April 22, 1205 was designated as the anniversary of Banda Aceh City) became the center of economic, political, social and cultural activities. On December 26, 2004, the city was hit by a tidal wave of a tsunami that killed hundreds of thousands of residents and destroyed more than 60% of the city's buildings. The Banda Aceh City area is located on the western tip of Sumatra Island, has an average land height of 0.80 meters above sea level stretching between 05° 16' 15' – 05° 36' 16” North Latitude and 95° 16' 15”-95 ° 22' 35” east longitude. In the north, Banda Aceh City is bordered by the Malacca Strait, while in the west it borders the Indonesian Ocean and in the east and south it borders the Aceh Besar Regency. The city of Banda Aceh administratively consists of 9 sub-districts and 88 villages or sub-districts. The area of ​​Banda Aceh is 61.36 km², with a population (2006) of 214,850 people, consisting of 112,129 males and 102,721 females. The strategic location of the area facing the Malacca Strait is a great potential in addition to various natural resources, both flora and fauna, tourism, ferry ports and fisheries to improve the community's economy.
